Here's what the great jurist Oliver Wendell Holmes, Sr. had to say about puns:

A pun does not commonly justify a blow in return. But if a blow were given for such cause, and death ensued, the jury would be judges both of the facts and of the pun, and might, if the latter were of an aggravated character, return a verdict of justifiable homicide.

Clearly, the dude was not a pun fan.

Whether you love or hate people who pun, you can't argue with Ron Wolfe, who said that A punster is someone who shticks by his word.
